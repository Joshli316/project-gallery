# CareNavigator California — Instructional Script

## What Is CareNavigator California?

CareNavigator California is a free web app that helps California families find disability benefits and government programs they qualify for. Instead of spending hours researching 100+ programs across federal, state, county, and nonprofit sources, families answer a short quiz and get personalized matches in about 15 minutes.

## Who Is It For?

This tool is designed for California families — especially in the Los Angeles metro area — who have a family member with a disability. It serves:

- Parents navigating benefits for a child with special needs
- Adults with disabilities looking for housing, healthcare, or financial assistance
- Family caregivers who need support programs
- Social workers and case managers helping clients find resources

## The Problem It Solves

Navigating disability benefits in California is overwhelming. There are over 100 programs spread across federal agencies, California state programs, LA County services, nonprofit organizations, and even local churches. Each has its own eligibility rules, application process, and deadlines.

Most families don't know what they qualify for. They either miss benefits they're entitled to, or they spend dozens of hours researching — time they don't have.

CareNavigator California cuts through this complexity. Answer a few questions about your family, and the app matches you to the programs most likely to help.

## How It Works — Step by Step

### Step 1: Start the Assessment

When you open the app, you'll see a landing page that explains what it does. Click "Start Free Assessment" to begin. No account required — your data stays in your browser.

### Step 2: Answer the Quiz

The quiz asks about your family situation:

- Where in California do you live? (County selection)
- How many people are in your household?
- What is your approximate household income?
- What type of disability is involved?
- What kinds of help are you looking for? (Healthcare, housing, financial, food, etc.)

The questions are straightforward — no medical jargon. The app is available in both English and Chinese (中文), with a language toggle in the top-right corner.

### Step 3: View Your Results

After completing the quiz, you'll see a personalized results page showing:

- **Programs you're likely eligible for** (70%+ match) — shown with a green confidence score
- **Programs you may qualify for** (40-70% match) — shown with a yellow score
- **Estimated monthly value** — an approximate dollar amount of benefits

Each program card shows the program name, what it provides, and your eligibility confidence percentage.

### Step 4: Explore Program Details

Click on any program to learn more:

- What the program provides
- Who qualifies
- How to apply
- Where to apply
- Estimated benefit amount

### The Program Database

CareNavigator California covers over 100 programs organized into categories:

- **26 California state programs** — Medi-Cal, CalFresh (SNAP), CalWORKs, SSI/SSP, IHSS, Regional Center services, and more
- **47 LA-area nonprofit organizations** — offering disability services, respite care, therapy, job training
- **15 churches and faith-based organizations** — providing community support and assistance
- **11 federal programs** — Social Security, Medicare, HUD housing vouchers
- **5 housing programs** — Section 8, supportive housing, affordable housing waitlists

### Key Features

- **Free and private** — no account required, no data collected, everything stays in your browser
- **Bilingual** — full English and Chinese (中文) support
- **Smart matching** — the eligibility engine uses your household size, income, county, and disability type to calculate match scores using Federal Poverty Level thresholds
- **Mobile-friendly** — works on phones, tablets, and desktops
- **Fast** — the full assessment takes about 15 minutes

## Why This Matters

Families dealing with disability often face an information gap. They don't know what help exists, or they assume they don't qualify. CareNavigator California closes that gap by putting all the relevant programs in one place and filtering them based on each family's specific situation.

The goal is simple: no California family with a disabled member should miss benefits they're entitled to because they didn't know where to look.
