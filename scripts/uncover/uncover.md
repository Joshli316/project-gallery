# Uncover — Instructional Script

## What Is Uncover?

Uncover is a visual conversation tool designed for facilitators who work with international students. It uses 30 carefully selected photographs and 4 questions inspired by Internal Family Systems (IFS) therapy to help a group of students open up about what they're actually carrying — homesickness, identity tension, cultural adjustment, loneliness — in a structured, safe, 20-minute exercise.

## Who Is It For?

Uncover is built for **facilitators**, not individual students. The people who get the most value from it are:

- Campus ministers and missionaries working with international student groups
- International Student and Scholar Services (ISSS) advisors
- Counselors and therapists running group sessions
- Orientation leaders at universities
- Anyone who leads small groups with international students

## The Problem It Solves

Facilitators who work with international students face a recurring challenge: how do you get a group of guarded, homesick students — many from cultures where emotional vulnerability isn't the norm — to actually talk about what's going on beneath the surface?

Most icebreakers are too shallow. Most therapy tools are too intense. Facilitators need something in between — a 20-minute exercise that builds trust, opens real conversation, and works in an official university setting (not just a faith context).

The closest existing tool is Soularium by Cru, but it's explicitly gospel-oriented, which limits its use in secular university programs. Uncover fills this gap with a secular, IFS-inspired framing that works in any context.

## How It Works — The Facilitator Experience

### Before the Session

1. **Visit the Uncover website** and review the exercise format
2. **Download the Session Kit** — a printable 6-page PDF with everything you need: setup instructions, the 4 questions, discussion prompts, and facilitation tips
3. **Prepare your space** — arrange chairs in a circle, set up a laptop or tablet to display photos, or print selected photos

### During the Session (20 Minutes)

**Opening (2 minutes):**
The facilitator explains the exercise: "I'm going to show you some photographs. For each one, I'll ask a question. There are no right or wrong answers. Just share what comes up for you."

**The Four Questions:**

Each question is paired with a set of photographs. Students look at the photos and respond:

1. **"Which photo catches your eye?"** — This is a low-stakes warm-up. Students just pick a photo they're drawn to and say why. Gets everyone talking.

2. **"Which photo reminds you of home?"** — This opens the door to homesickness, cultural identity, and memory. Students often share specific stories about family, places, or traditions.

3. **"Which photo shows how you feel right now?"** — This is the emotional core. Students use the photos as a safe proxy to express feelings they might not say directly: loneliness, overwhelm, hope, confusion.

4. **"Which photo represents where you want to be?"** — This is forward-looking. Students articulate their hopes, goals, and vision for their time abroad.

**Closing (3 minutes):**
The facilitator summarizes themes that came up, affirms what was shared, and invites anyone who wants to continue the conversation to connect afterward.

### The Photos

The 30 photographs are curated to be:
- **Culturally neutral** — no text, no specific cultural symbols that would alienate anyone
- **Emotionally evocative** — landscapes, objects, people in contemplative moments, abstract textures
- **Open to interpretation** — each photo can mean different things to different people, which is the point

### Using the Web App

The web app at uncover.yellow-longitudinal.workers.dev has two main paths:

**For facilitators (the main audience):**
- Landing page explains the tool and who it's for
- "Download Session Kit" button provides the printable PDF
- Overview of the exercise format and facilitation tips

**Try It Yourself (solo mode):**
- Click "Try It Yourself" to experience the question flow individually
- Browse through photos, select ones that resonate, reflect on each question
- Useful for facilitators to preview the experience before running it with a group

## Why This Works

Uncover works because of three design principles:

1. **Photos lower the barrier.** Asking "how do you feel?" gets awkward silence. Asking "which photo shows how you feel?" gives students a concrete thing to point at and talk about. The photo becomes a safe object to project emotions onto.

2. **The question sequence builds depth gradually.** Question 1 is easy (just pick a photo). Question 2 adds personal context (home). Question 3 goes emotional (right now). Question 4 turns hopeful (the future). By the time you hit Question 3, the group has already been talking for 10 minutes and feels safe enough to go deeper.

3. **The IFS framing validates complexity.** Internal Family Systems recognizes that people carry multiple "parts" — you can feel excited about studying abroad AND homesick AND anxious AND grateful, all at the same time. Uncover's photo-based format naturally surfaces this multiplicity without requiring students to label their emotions in a second language.
