# CareerReady — Instructional Script

## What Is CareerReady?

CareerReady is a free web app that walks college graduates through every step of the job search — from figuring out their strengths to landing their first job. It's a connected toolkit of 8 sections and over 25 interactive tools, all in one place. Write once, and your information auto-fills everywhere.

## Who Is It For?

- College graduates entering the job market for the first time
- International students navigating U.S. job search norms
- Career center staff who need a self-service tool for students
- Anyone who feels overwhelmed by the scattered mess of job prep

The app is fully bilingual — English and Chinese (中文) — and requires no sign-up. All data is saved locally in your browser.

## The Problem It Solves

Job prep is scattered across dozens of different tools with no clear path. Students use one site for resumes, another for interview prep, another for job boards — and nothing connects. They don't know where to start, they repeat the same information everywhere, and they lose track of their progress.

CareerReady solves this by organizing the entire job search into three clear phases with tools that talk to each other.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Know Yourself

This is the foundation. Before you write a resume or walk into an interview, you need to understand what you bring to the table. Phase 1 has six tools:

**1. Transferable Skills Inventory** — Start here. Check off your soft skills (communication, leadership, professionalism) and hard skills (technology, business, healthcare, creative). You can also add custom skills. Everything you select here flows into your resume and interview prep automatically.

**2. Work Values** — Rank what matters most to you in a workplace: salary, flexibility, purpose, team culture, growth opportunities. This helps you filter job opportunities later.

**3. Challenge Your Beliefs** — Job searching triggers negative thinking: "I'm not qualified enough," "Nobody will hire me." This tool helps you identify those thoughts and replace them with evidence-based positive alternatives.

**4. Goal Setting with FOCUS** — Set concrete, measurable job search goals using the FOCUS method. Instead of "I want a job," you'll write goals like "Apply to 5 marketing coordinator roles in the Bay Area by March 15."

**5. Personal Branding** — Write your brand statement — a short paragraph summarizing who you are professionally. This becomes the Profile section of your resume automatically.

**6. Power Statement** — Craft a 30-second elevator pitch for networking events and interviews. When someone asks "So what do you do?" you'll have a confident, rehearsed answer.

### Phase 2: Market Your Brand

Now that you know yourself, it's time to present yourself to employers.

**Applications Section:**
- **10 Essential Application Tips** — How to get past the first screening
- **Experience Gap Solver** — Turn volunteer work, freelance projects, and internships into strong application material
- **Master Application Builder** — A central form with all your personal info so you can fill any application fast

**Resumes Section:**
- **Resume Builder** — Build your resume with a form-based editor. Choose from three templates (Chronological, Functional, Combination). Your skills and branding from Phase 1 auto-fill in. Export to PDF when done.
- **Cover Letter Builder** — Write targeted cover letters matched to specific job postings
- **Reference Page** — Create a formatted reference page that matches your resume style
- **Email Guide** — Templates for emailing your resume to employers professionally

**Interviews Section:**
- **STAR Storybank** — The most powerful tool in the app. Write behavioral interview stories using the Situation/Task/Action/Result method. Rate each story, tag it with skills, and track which of the 15 most common behavioral questions your stories cover. The goal is 6-8 polished stories.
- **JD Decoder** — Paste a full job description and the app analyzes what the employer is really looking for, then cross-references your storybank to flag gaps
- **AI Mock Interview** — Practice answering interview questions and get coaching feedback
- **Common Questions** — Draft answers to the 8 most-asked interview questions
- **Company Research** — Organize research on each company before your interview
- **Thank You Notes** — Write post-interview thank-you notes the same day

**Job Search Section:**
- **Networking** — List people who can help and plan your outreach
- **Job Boards & Websites** — Where to search online with tips for each platform
- **Job Search Checklist** — Track progress across all search methods

**Social Media Section:**
- **Online Presence Audit** — Review your social media profiles through an employer's eyes
- **Platform Quick Tips** — Actionable advice for LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook

### Phase 3: Prove Yourself

You got the job — now what?

- **Workplace Success** — Tips for thriving in your first 90 days
- **Self-Evaluation** — Monthly performance tracking to stay on top of your growth

**Contact Log** — A mini-CRM that tracks every company you've applied to, every interview, and every follow-up in one dashboard.

## The Key Differentiator: Write Once, Auto-Fill Everywhere

The reason CareerReady works as a connected system instead of 25 separate tools:

- Skills you identify in Phase 1 automatically populate your Resume Builder
- Your brand statement becomes your resume's Profile section
- Your STAR stories prepare you for mock interviews
- Your contact log tracks the full lifecycle from application to offer

You never re-enter the same information twice.
